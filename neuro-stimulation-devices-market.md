<p><p>The neurostimulation devices market comprises various technologies designed to modulate nerve activity. Deep-brain stimulation (DBS) targets specific brain regions for conditions like Parkinson's disease. Vagus nerve stimulation (VNS) involves electrical impulses to the vagus nerve, aiding in epilepsy and depression. Responsive neurostimulation (RNS) detects and responds to seizures in real-time. Transcranial magnetic stimulation (TMS) uses magnetic fields to stimulate nerve cells, primarily for depression. Spinal cord stimulation (SCS) alleviates chronic pain, while peripheral nerve stimulation (PNS) targets peripheral nerves for pain management.</p></p>
