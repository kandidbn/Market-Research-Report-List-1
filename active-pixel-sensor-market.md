<p><ul><li>Buried Photodiode-structure</li><li>Selection Transistor</li><li>MOS Amplifier</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The active-pixel sensor (APS) market features three primary types: buried photodiode structures, selection transistors, and MOS amplifiers. Buried photodiodes enable improved light sensitivity and integration into smaller pixel designs, enhancing image quality. Selection transistors act as switches, allowing individual pixel readout and reducing noise. MOS amplifiers amplify the signal directly at the pixel level, leading to better performance in low-light conditions. Together, these components contribute to advancements in imaging technologies, making APS a preferred choice in various applications, including smartphones and digital cameras.</p></p>
