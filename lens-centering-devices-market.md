<p><p>Lens centering devices are essential for ensuring accurate alignment in optical systems. The market comprises two primary types: mechanical alignment techniques, which utilize physical adjustments to achieve precise lens positioning; and active optical assembly techniques, which employ sensors and feedback systems for real-time alignment corrections. Mechanical methods often rely on rigid fixtures, while active systems adapt dynamically to environmental changes. Both approaches aim to optimize optical performance and image quality in various applications, including telecommunications, imaging systems, and consumer electronics.</p></p>
