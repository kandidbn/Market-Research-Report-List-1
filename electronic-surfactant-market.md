<p><p>The electronic surfactant market is categorized into anionic, cationic, nonionic, amphoteric, and others. Anionic surfactants, negatively charged, are effective in cleaning and foaming agents. Cationic surfactants, positively charged, excel in antimicrobial properties and conditioning. Nonionic surfactants, with no charge, provide stability and low toxicity, often used in cosmetics and detergents. Amphoteric surfactants can exhibit both positive and negative charges, offering versatility in formulations. The "others" category encompasses specialized surfactants for niche applications.</p></p>
